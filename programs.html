Our Programming Season

The Dante Society's programming year runs from September through August, with meetings held on the third Tuesday of each month from September through May. Summer months feature special gatherings and local excursions.

Programs include lectures on Italian-related topics, musical performances, film screenings, cooking demonstrations, wine tastings, and Carnevale celebrations. We also partner with Opera on the James, the Maier Museum of Art, and other area organizations for special events.

Unless otherwise noted, programs are held at Westminster Canterbury, 3rd Floor Commons at 4:00 PM, with optional dinner following at a local Italian restaurant.

Programs & Events — 2026–2027 Season

January 2026

Milano's Italian Dinner

An evening of Italian cuisine and camaraderie at Milano's Restaurant. Members and guests enjoyed a wonderful start to the 2026 season with delicious food and great company.

February 2026

Carnevale Celebration

A festive evening celebrating the Italian Carnevale tradition with masks, music, traditional treats, and entertainment. A highlight of the Society's social calendar.

April 2026

Dante in Art — Lecture by Jerry Carney

A fascinating lecture exploring Dante Alighieri's profound influence on visual art, from Renaissance manuscript illuminations of The Divine Comedy to contemporary interpretations. Jerry Carney led members through centuries of artistic responses to Dante's masterpiece.

May 19, 2026

Film: Valentino: The Last Emperor

A screening of the acclaimed documentary at Westminster Canterbury, followed by dinner at Isabella's Restaurant. The film documents the life and career of legendary fashion designer Valentino Garavani.

Viva l'Italia — September 20, 2026

Our signature annual event celebrating Italian heritage. Membership dues are due by this date. Details of this year's celebration will be announced soon — stay tuned!

Ongoing

Monthly Film & Dinner Series

Each month, the Society screens an Italian-themed film followed by dinner at a local Italian restaurant. Films range from classic Italian cinema to documentaries about Italian arts, history, and culture. Dinner reservations are coordinated by Pat Bradbury.

Ongoing

Collaborations with Local Organizations

We regularly partner with Opera on the James, the Maier Museum of Art, and other cultural organizations for special programs. Members receive advance notice and discounted tickets where available.

Program Format & Email Reminders

Program reminders are sent by email approximately one week before each event. Each reminder includes:

  • Event date, time, and location
  • Program description and featured speaker/performer
  • Dinner details and reservation contact
  • Parking instructions and directions
  • RSVP information
